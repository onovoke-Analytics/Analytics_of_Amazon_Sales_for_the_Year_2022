# Analytics_of_Amazon_Sales_for_the_Year_2022
Introduction

In today’s data-driven business environment, organizations rely heavily on Business Intelligence (BI) dashboards to monitor performance, identify trends and support strategic decision-making. This project focuses on the analysis of Amazon sales data for the year 2022 using an interactive dashboard developed with modern data visualization techniques.
The dashboard provides insights into major sales indicators such as Total Profit, Total Sales, Total Orders and Total Quantity Sold. It also explores customer regions, product categories, payment methods, weekday versus weekend sales patterns and hourly purchasing behavior.

The purpose of this analysis is to transform raw sales data into actionable business intelligence that can support operational efficiency, customer engagement and revenue optimization strategies.

Potential Insights

The dashboard revealed several valuable potential business insights, including:

•	Identification of top-performing product categories 

•	Discovery of customer purchasing trends 

•	Recognition of regional revenue drivers 

•	Understanding of customer payment preferences 

•	Detection of sales peak periods 

•	Identification of underperforming categories 

•	Recognition of operational opportunities for targeted marketing 

These insights can improve strategic planning, inventory management and customer engagement initiatives.

In-Analysis Observations

The analysis was conducted at two levels: the overall annual performance for the 2022 business year and the monthly dashboard analysis filtered to March 2022 through the dashboard slicer. This approach provides both a broad business overview and a more detailed monthly performance perspective.

Monthly Dashboard Performance – March 2022:
The dashboard slicer was filtered to March 2022 to provide a more focused monthly analysis of Amazon’s sales performance.
During March 2022, Amazon recorded approximately $1.61 million in total sales and generated approximately $69.63K in profit. The platform also processed about 2,155 customer orders with approximately 6,455 units sold within the month.
The dashboard further revealed that weekday sales significantly outperformed weekend sales, suggesting that customers were more active during regular business days. This may be influenced by work-related purchasing behavior, promotional timing or increased weekday online engagement.

Among the product categories, the Beauty category emerged as the highest-performing segment during the month, followed closely by Sports, Fashion and Electronics. This indicates strong customer interest in lifestyle and consumer-focused products.

The analysis also showed that digital wallet payments accounted for the highest transaction values, reflecting growing customer preference for cashless and digital payment solutions.
In terms of customer purchasing behavior, shopping activity peaked during the late evening hours, particularly between 9 PM and midnight, indicating that customers were more likely to shop during leisure periods after work hours.

Finally, the daily sales trend remained relatively stable throughout March 2022, with occasional spikes likely associated with promotional campaigns, discounts or periods of increased customer demand.

Overall Annual Business Performance (2022 Dataset Overview):

The complete 2022 dataset revealed that Amazon generated approximately $18.91 million in total sales, reflecting strong market demand and extensive customer engagement across multiple product categories and regions.
The company also achieved approximately $819.47K in total profit, indicating healthy operational performance and positive profitability throughout the business year.

Customer activity remained significantly high, with a total of 24,926 completed orders and approximately 74,738 units sold across the platform during the reporting period. This demonstrates strong transactional volume and sustained customer purchasing behavior throughout 2022.

Overall, the annual analysis reflects a stable and scalable e-commerce operation supported by diversified product offerings, broad regional participation and consistent customer demand.

Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:
Increase Focus on High-Performing Categories: Amazon should allocate more marketing resources and inventory support to top-performing categories such as Beauty and Sports.
Optimize Weekday Campaigns: Since weekday sales dominate overall revenue, targeted weekday promotions can further improve sales performance.

Improve Weekend Engagement: Weekend-specific campaigns, discounts and flash sales may help boost lower weekend transactions.
Expand Digital Payment Incentives: Given the popularity of wallet payments, offering cashback incentives and digital payment rewards can encourage higher transaction volumes.

Strengthen Regional Strategies: Regions with lower performance should receive localized marketing campaigns and product customization strategies.

Utilize Peak Hour Marketing: Targeted advertisements and push notifications should be scheduled during peak purchasing hours to maximize conversion rates.
